This project was started as for my university class “Medien Informatik 2” in my second semester of University.

We were assigned to make a small game, in a group, using the Phaser HTML5 game engine for JavaScript, while preparing documentation and applying learned information from the university lectures.

A GIF of a Phaser Game Projects Level Editor.

Gameplay wise, the player would control a drone which could only grapple along walls and jump off them. The play has to navigate through a large map and beat 3-5 bosses, in any order, to beat the game. Inspired by the Game Titan Souls, these bosses would function like an action puzzle box, and both the player and the boss would die in one hit. Death would only be a minor setback of being thrown out of the boos arena and the boss resetting. The player had to learn the behaviour of a boss, while trying to survive and position themselves in a way they could beat the boss, while managing their own survival.

The game was a combination of many excellent design choices from inspiring works across mediums.

The two aspects I was excited to explore most was firstly, to give the game a sci-fi, industrial + jungle theme but to completely avoid showing the jungle part.

The Japanese animation film Akira from 1991 has a similar theme for the city the film takes place in, but the jungle aspect is only suggested by the music and the portrayed city having many aspects in common that we associate with a jungle ecosystem. The metropolis being crowded, loud and dense, not a plant in sight, and jet together with the tribal music the subconscious association with a thick jungle is immediately apparent. Second, we wanted to try to recreate the timeless intrigue, a vast and purposefully designed world like the map of Shadow of the Colossus. Where space and exploration are removed from the usual player reward process and have meaning in themselves.

They were some management mistakes on the universities side this semester, so the time we had for all the projects was not appropriate. Resulting in a lot of crunch on our side and changes in deadlines and evaluation criteria on theirs.

In the end we had a basic level editor, some base mechanics and some good visual assets. I felt we hadn't done the vision of the project justice, so I would like to continue the development. But other courses and some earned holiday has priority, if my schedule allows it I would like to complete at least the tutorial section of the game.
